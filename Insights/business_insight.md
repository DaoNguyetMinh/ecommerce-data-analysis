\### BUSINESS INSIGHT



\## 1. Marketplace Growth

&#x09;Marketplace revenue grew rapidly between 2016 and 2018. Revenue increased from 51,813 in 2016 to over 8.5 million in 2018, representing a dramatic expansion of the platform.

&#x09;The largest growth occurred between 2016 and 2017, when revenue increased by more than 13,000% indicating the early scaling phase of the marketplace.

\## 2. Revenue Structure

&#x09;Revenue is highly distributed across the product catalog. The top-performing product contributes only 0.43% of total revenue, while the top 10 products together account for just 3.21%.

This indicates that the marketplace follows a long-tail sales pattern, where a large number of products collectively generate revenue rather than relying on a few dominant best sellers.

\-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

* Revenue by category: Revenue is relatively diversified across product categories. The top 5 categories contribute approximately 39% of total revenue, with no single category exceeding 10%. this indicates that the business dose not rely heavily on a specific category and benefits from a balanced product mix. Such diversification may reduce risk but also suggests that growth opportunities could come from optimizing multiple categories rather than focusing on a single dominant segment.

\## 3. Seller Contribution

&#x09;Analysis of the top 5 sellers by revenue shows that 4 of them are located in the state of São Paulo (SP). this suggests that high-performing sellers are geographically concentrated in this region.

&#x09;The dominance of São Paulo sellers may reflect stronger logistics networks, better access to suppliers, or higher marketplace adoption in this area.

\## 4. Customer Contribution

&#x09;A small number of customers generate the highest revenue through repeated or high-value purchases. Retaining these high-value customers could be important for maintaining revenue stability.
